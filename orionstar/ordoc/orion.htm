Overview

The Orion Star Sports & Outdoors sample data is a fictitious case study that helps you learn to use and exploit SAS Software, and to experience how the SAS Intelligence Architecture components can be utilized together to provide a powerful solution demonstrating the intelligence value chain. Also, SAS software documentation uses this data as a basis for examples. Try out the samples and examples yourself to learn about what you can accomplish and how to do it!

The Orion Star Company

The fictional Orion Sports & Outdoors is an international retail company that sells sports and outdoor products. The headquarters is based in the United States, and retail stores are situated in a number of other countries including Belgium, Holland, Germany, the United Kingdom, Denmark, France, Italy, Spain, and Australia.

 

Products are sold through physical retail stores, as well as through mail-order catalogs and the Internet. Customers who sign up as members of the Orion Star Club organization can receive favorable special offers; therefore, most customers enroll in the Orion Star Club. The sales data in the scenario only include the purchases by Orion Star Club members in the years 1998 through 2002.

The Employees and Organizational Structure

The United States hosts the headquarters as well as most of the administrative functions. A large number of the approximately 600–800 employees are also stationed in the United States. Catalog sales and Internet sales, import, export, shipping and stock handling are centralized functions administered from the headquarters, but some staff also works through Orion Star offices in the various countries.

 

Each country has a retail company associated with it; these are named Orion Belgium, Orion Spain, and so on. These country-specific branches each have their own sales staff. All offices have been operational within the whole time range of the data (1998 through 2002), except the Belgian office that was opened in 1999.

 

The employees are identified in the database according to a five-level hierarchy:

 

  • Country (where the employee is based)
  • Company (Orion Country-name, or a centralized function such as “Logistics”)
  • Department
  • Section
  • Group

 

Other employee information in the sample includes the following:

  • Hire and termination dates
  • Start and end dates for a specific job title
  • Address
  • Gender
  • Salary
  • Manager

The Merchandise

Approximately 5500 different sports and outdoors products are offered at Orion Star. Some of the products are not sold in certain countries, whereas others are sold in volumes that reflect the different types of sports and outdoor activities that are performed in each country. All of the product names are fictitious.

 

Products are organized in a hierarchy consisting of four levels:

  • Product Line
  • Product Category
  • Product Group
  • Product

 

Each product has a cost price and a sales price. All prices are in U.S. dollars. By using a start-date and an end-date for prices in the price list, price changes over time are recorded and the history of price changes is maintained. The data also includes discount prices for certain products at certain times. However, product prices are similar around the world.

The Customers

Orion Star’s customers are spread across many countries, including, but not limited to, the countries with Orion Star stores. Customer names as well as street addresses are fictitious, but real city names, county/region names and country names are used.

 

Approximately 90,000 customers are included in the scenario (though not all of them are active buyers). The Address information for a particular customer is stored in a number of tables that contain some or all of the following basic geographical information:

  • Street
  • Postal code
  • City
  • County
  • Region
  • State
  • Country
  • Continent

 

This data is controlled by means of pointers (ID columns), as often seen where geographical data is gathered from official sources that are maintained externally to the organization. This approach facilitates changes to such information as postal codes and street names. Customers are placed into customer groups according to their buying activity.

The Orders

Most of the orders that are included in the scenario are sales to Orion Star Club members for whom customer information is recorded. Retail sales orders have a pointer to the sales person. Approximately 980,000 orders are in the data, and the buying patterns reflect seasonal, geographical and sports traditions. Each order contains one or more order lines—one line per product purchased.

The Suppliers

All of the Orion Star products in the database have a pointer to a supplier. Suppliers are based in different countries, but the U.S.-based Orion Star purchasing organization does all purchasing. There are 64 suppliers, but only one supplier per product.

The SAS Users at Orion Star

The Orion Star company licenses SAS software and uses it in a number of departments. The IS (Information Systems) department extracts data from its Online Transactions Systems (OLTP) and Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems, and also obtains data from external sources. The IS department has built a data warehouse and data marts, using SAS software, that enables the business users to perform query and reporting, advanced analyses, and so forth. In addition, the Applications Development team in the IS department has used SAS software to create a number of intranet-based applications for management information. The business users include C-level executives, business analysts, and middle management.

 

The Data Models

 

The Orion Star data is organized into two different stages of the intelligence value chain, namely before entering a data warehousing process and after:

  • The relational normalized data model represents the extracts from OLTP, ERP, and other systems. This data can be transformed, cleansed and loaded into a data warehouse or data mart.
  • The dimensional model represents a data mart that was created as a result of the above process. This model constitutes a star schema design, and it is well suited for use in business intelligence applications as well as for ad hoc query and reporting. This comes in two versions: A star schema containing all data rows, and a subset for “gold customers” with high and low activity and their orders of products in the Clothes & Shoes product line. The structure of the two star schemas is identical.

The Relational Normalized Data Model

This is supposed to be transformed, cleansed, and loaded into a data warehouse or data mart.  The program code that you need to transform data from the normalized model to the dimensional model is included in the /orpgm folder.

 

Relational Normalized Model

           

The normalized data model contains a number of Start_Date and End_Date columns. These are used to ensure an accurate history of changes (such as changes in prices for products, and so forth). Thus, an item can exist more than once in a particular table, but only once within a particular time interval.

 

The Product and Organization tables are organized hierarchically. They use product-level and organization-level IDs to determine the placement of an entity within the appropriate hierarchy.

 

Geographical data varies in structure and quality from country to country. Some of the geographical tables are populated with data only from certain countries.

The Dimensional Data Model

The dimensional data model is the result of an ETL (Extract, Transform and Load) process and represents one possible data mart. The dimensional model is a star schema with a fact table that contains orders. The dimension tables represent data for Time, Geography, Customers, Products, Suppliers and Employees.

 

Dimensional Data Model

 

            To ease queries and analysis, some of the columns have been duplicated in multiple tables, for example, Country.

The Data Dictionary

A data dictionary containing column definitions is supplied with the data in the file /ordoc/contents.html  (The /orpgm/ordict.sas program generates this output, and contains additional code to document SAS formats used).

Sample Programs

A number of sample SAS programs performing queries, reports, utilities and conversions etc. on the Orion Star data are enclosed in the /orpgm folder.

 

Installation and Deployment of Orion Star Samples and Data

 

You may need to modify the location of root folder of the scenario by editing the /ormacro/orion.sas.

 

Under the /orion folder, sub-folders and contents are as follows:

  • /ordetail     Data for the normalized model
  • /orstar       Data for the dimensional star schema model
  • /orgold       Data for the subset dimensional star schema model
  • /orsum       Summary data
  • /orformat   Formats for all of the data
  • /orpgm       Programs to create the dimensional model from the normalized model
  • /ordoc        Documentation for the data, the models and the case
  • /ormacro   Macro to set up libref’s to the Orion Star data

 

To use the scenario in a regular SAS session, insert the following statements at the beginning of your program:

 

            %include “your-path/orion/ormacro/orion.sas”;

%orion;

 

This macro sets up the required librefs as follows:

  • ordetail            Points to the /ordetail data of the normalized data
  • orstar              Points to the /orstar data of the star schema
  • orgold              Points to the /orgold data of the subset gold star schema
  • orsum              Points to the summary data in /orsum
  • orformat          Points to the format catalog orionfmt in /orformat folder
